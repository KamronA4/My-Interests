Kamron Aggor

Overview

In recent years, the general sentiment towards the cost of living in the United States has seen a significant shift.

Basic needs such as groceries, transportation, home utilities, etc. are all costs that have risen with time. However, the most significant component to Americans' cost of living which has hurt their quality of life the most is housing. As a Rhode Islander, I am all too familiar with stories in recent years about people who have lived in certain areas for generations being completely priced out of their homes. As someone who will eventually have to peruse the housing market myself, it is also in my best interest to be informed on the following:

  1. Understand what the housing climate looks like right now.

  2. Understand what things may look like in the near future.

  3. Understand how Rhode Island compares to other housing markets across the country.

To find an answer to these questions, I shall investigate the Rhode Island housing market using a data-forward approach.

Data

The dataset I have chosen for this investigation comes from Redfin.com's Monthly Housing Market Data within the 'public data center' portion of their website.

Aggor_Housing_Data

I found this dataset by looking up lists online for reputable realtor datasets, and found Redfin as one of the most consistently mentioned websites. After looking up Redfin.com's housing data, I browsed through the site until I found compelling data which I felt could help me answer my questions.

This data, which contains over 21,000 records, is updated on a monthly basis and is comprised of many different metrics regarding the housing market across the country. The headers specify region and the month for which the data was collected in, the median home sale price, the number of homes sold, the number of new listings, the total housing inventory, the number of days on the market these homes have been listed, and the average sale price. These metrics also include YoY and MoM calculations to compare the changes in the housing market over the short-term and long-term.

Firstly, I looked at charts from the Federal Reserve Economic Data website. These charts plotted key figures pertaining to housing affordability over time. As you may notice, each of ht metrics are increasing over time, with a steep and sudden increase appearing during 2020.
